I never thought I was smart.

Everyone cheered my name in praise and honor, but I never fully internalized their words. Despite never dipping below top 2 in my elementary years, I didn’t feel any smarter than my other classmates back then.

Come my high school years in Mandaue City Science High School, I witnessed truly brilliant minds, talents I couldn’t even approach, and geniuses of arts. I accepted, then, that I was no more than the average fish in the sea when compared to the sharks that surrounded me.

What separated me from the others, however, was that I asked. I raised questions. Out of curiosity. Out of a pure desire to learn. Out of eagerness. Out of love. I carried this idea with me throughout my high school years, up until this very moment. It kept me going through my college journey, and now that I have stood before students who might’ve thought that they aren’t smart, like me, I can be here for them, and tell them that:

“Better a fool now and ask, than remain a fool forever.”
