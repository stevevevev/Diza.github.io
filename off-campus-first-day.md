<h2> First Day</h2>
Our first day in CCNHS was definitely something. Most of us arrived early, I personally arrived at 6 A.M., but still couldn’t proceed because our school leader was running late (she’s a good leader, nonetheless) since she was still printing our documents. We waited in the Principal's office, chatting with one another until our leader arrived. When she handed over the required documents, we were called, specialization by specialization, until it was only us, the science majors left in the room. When it was our turn to be called, we were given a choice to pick which grade level we wanted. Initially, I was to handle the grade 7s, but I backtracked, and decided to handle the grade 10s, which turned out to be a good decision for me. Afterwards, I was told to wait for my mentor to arrive, and so I waited in front of the computer laboratory.

When Madame Guiroy arrived, she had the aura of a “terror teacher”, and I thought to myself “okay, this is gonna be a good internship experience” (spoiler alert: it was). She explained to me my roles, and responsibilities that I was going to undertake, and she had already assigned to me the topics that I was to teach, and the schedules for the sections that I was to handle. 6 grade 10 classes, back to back, with only a 15 minute lunch break in between was definitely a rough schedule, but at least my time in was 9 AM with a 3 pm timeout, the same as hers. That day she just tasked me with getting to know the students, and to give time to the students after so they can work on their films.

My first class was 9:30, Grade 10- Cabahug. I went in, and was immediately met with chaos. Chairs strewn everywhere, boys at the back playing with their phones, girls gossiping about something I don’t recall, typical classroom shenanigans that I was no stranger with. I had some students call their classmates who went out of the room, and when they were all settled in their seats, I just introduced myself, and gave them the time. It was here that I felt that something was wrong. If I could turn back time, I wish I had asked them to also introduce themselves, but at that time, I hadn’t thought about that, and so I sat in their classroom, fiddling with my phone, just waiting for time to pass so I can get to my next class.

10:15 arrived, and I was in Grade 10 - Ibona, whose students felt a bit more disciplined, as they were in their seats when I came in. The proper atmosphere made me think clearly, and it was here that I had realized I should also get to know my students, so I had them introduce themselves to me. I gave them the remaining time for their films afterward.

At 11:00 A.M., I found myself in Grade 10 - Yap, and I had a similar experience with Ibona. I got to know my students, then gave them the remaining time.
11:45 came, my last class for the morning, Grade 10 - Montalban, and it was a similar experience with the last 2 sections I went into.
My morning classes ended at 12:30, and it was time for my lunch break… which I never used to eat my lunch. The 15 minute reprise felt more like a breather, and I’m thankful for it, though it did always leave me wanting for more.

12:45 P.M. had come, but my next class, Grade 10 - Villanueva, still hadn’t. A significant portion of the class still had not arrived, so much so that I thought their classes would start later. I asked the students, and they told me they usually started their classes at 1, which I naively believed. With the few that have arrived, I chatted, and got to know them, the same as the other sections. Slowly, however, their class started to fill up with students arriving one by one. 

1:30 arrived, and my last class for the afternoon was ready for me. Grade 10 - Rago was definitely the class with the most number of exceptional students, and I thought then that it would be the section that I would be looking forward to the most (spoiler alert: I was wrong). 

Finally, my classes ended at 2:15 P.M., and after a quick chat with my mentor, I was free to do my business, and go home at 3:00 P.M., which I did.

My first day of getting to know my classes felt a little satisfying, and I would relive that day if I could. Writing this only makes me miss my students more.
