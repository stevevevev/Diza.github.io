## Expectations

So, the last phase of my internship is finally here: my on-campus deployment back in ILS. Unfortunately, it’s still going to be online, which is a bit of a bummer. But honestly, I feel a lot more prepared for it now. I’m bringing in the solid foundation I built during pre-deployment and combining it with the real-world survival skills and insights I picked up during my off-campus journey. Plus, another exciting context: I recently got my drawing tablet! It's a huge game-changer, and I can finally write and draw figures a lot better when presenting to the students.

Here is what I am expecting and what I hope to achieve during this final stretch.

My Expectations
A Familiar Environment with the Same Online Hurdles: Even though I'm back on my home turf at ILS, I expect the online setup to still have its usual challenges—like screen fatigue and quiet virtual rooms. However, after my off-campus experience, I expect to handle these moments with a lot more patience and less panic.

A Need for Flexibility: I expect that things won't always go exactly as planned. Tech issues happen, and sometimes a lesson takes longer than expected. I'm coming in expecting to adapt on the fly, just like I had to do in my previous deployment.

Higher Standards from Myself: Since this is the final phase, I expect my cooperating teachers to look closely at how much I've grown. More importantly, I expect myself to teach with more confidence than I did on day one.

## Goals

1. Maximize My New Drawing Tablet
Instead of just clicking through static PowerPoint slides, my biggest goal is to make my lessons feel more alive. Now that I have my drawing tablet, I want to actively write out notes, draw figures, and solve problems live on screen. It’s going to make presenting so much better and help the students actually see my thought process, making the online class feel a little more like a traditional whiteboard session.

2. Blend Pre-Deployment Theory with Off-Campus Reality
During pre-deployment, I learned the "ideal" way to make a lesson plan and handle a class. Off-campus taught me what to do when reality hits and that ideal goes out the window. My goal is to merge these two: I want to keep the strong, pedagogical structure from my early learnings, but apply the empathy, pacing, and realistic adjustments I learned while teaching off-campus.

3. Keep the Students Engaged Despite the Screen
Since we are unfortunately still online, it’s so easy for students to zone out. I want to use my new visual tools and the confidence I've built to keep the energy up. I want to make sure I’m checking in on them, asking good questions, and making the virtual classroom feel like a space where they actually want to participate and learn.

4. Finish Strong and Solidify My Teaching Style
This is the last stop before I finish the internship. My ultimate goal is to figure out exactly who I am as a teacher. I want to leave ILS knowing I gave it my best shot, connected with my students, and proved that I can deliver a great lesson—even if it has to be through a screen.
