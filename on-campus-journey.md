<div style="text-align: justify; margin-bottom: 40px;">
  <h2>Experience</h2>
  
  <p>This time around, there’s only 6 of us deployed to handle the ILS classes: Teacher Euniece, Scott, Genesis, Lovely, Cezel, and I. With only the 6 of us, we were split into 3 pairs, each taking a subject before rotating to handle other subjects.</p>

  <h3>Values 4.1</h3>
  <p>My first lesson assignment, together with Teacher Scott, was “Developing Prudence to Balance Positive Dispositions” for Values 8. Armed with a better grasp of the art of questioning, together with my drawing tablet, I was able to capture their attention, and enhance participation. This time around, I encouraged the use of reacts and the chatbox for participation, instead of shying away from them. I still greatly appreciated those who answered vocally, but I welcomed all forms of participation now.</p>

  <h3>Math 4.2</h3>
  <p>The next lesson me, and teacher Scott were assigned to was “GMDAS on Fractions” in Math 5. This posed a new challenge to me, because this was a pre-recorded video for the students’ consumption. This method directly contradicts everything that I’ve been developing thus far, a pre-recorded video has 0 interactions, the teacher becomes the giver of knowledge, I cannot test student mastery without any kind of interaction because my way of evaluation comes from my newly developed, and refined art of questioning. Nevertheless, I pulled through, and acted like a youtuber, or at least emulated how youtubers typically talked to retain the attention of the children.</p>

  <h3>Science 4.3</h3>
  <p>The next lesson assignment my partner and I got was “Kinetic and Potential Energy” for Science 8. This time, it was synchronous, and with my drawing tablet, plus my art of questioning, I was praised for my impressive student-centered approach in teaching involving discovery. I truly became a guide, instead of a dispenser of knowledge. I took the words of my students, affirmed or corrected them, and synthesized their answers to form the bigger picture. The confidence I built from my off-campus deployment certainly helped.</p>

  <p>My first set of classes set the tone for the rest of my on-campus journey. Most of my expectations I was able to meet and exceed. My learnings from my pre-deployment, together with the lessons I gained from my off-campus journey transformed my teaching in ways my old self never could have imagined.</p>
</div>
