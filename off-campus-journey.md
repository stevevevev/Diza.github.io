<h2> The Journey </h2>

Throughout my off-campus internship, my classroom management skills, and my art of questioning only deepened. One part of me that I didn’t expect to develop, however, was also my ability to connect. My mentor instilled in me that teachers should have concrete boundaries within their classrooms, but otherwise free to do whatever outside. I internalized my mentor’s guidance, and set up personal boundaries, making sure the students feel that I am their teacher, and they are my students. Paradoxically, however, I feel that I was able to connect better with them in that way. I was able to have more fun that way. I was able to enjoy our classes better that way. 


With all that said, I do also want to apologize, and thank my first section, Grade 10 - Cabahug, for serving as my guinea pigs in improving my teaching process of the day. As my first section, I experimented with their section on how I could effectively deliver the lessons that I was going to teach. I’d also like to thank Grade 10 - Ibona for responding respectfully to my methods. I could affirm or correct the modifications I made for how I taught the lesson in Cabahug. Grade 10 - Yap made me want to challenge them, and I’d like to apologize to them for making my questions a little bit harder. Grade 10 - Montalban was definitely a fun experience, though quite a little difficult, but I still want to thank them for trying their best. 


Unexpectedly, it was Grade 10 - Villanueva that really took my heart. I was able to connect with them more because I could talk to them individually (since a lot of them were usually late), and one thing I noticed was that as time went on, less and less of them were getting late in my class. Unsurprisingly, it was Grade 10 - Rago that allowed me to go to my 100% in teaching. As my last class, they were able to benefit from me experimenting with different teaching methods from all the other classes I had. 


I truly did not expect to have enjoyed teaching that much, and my experience only deepened my love for teaching. I thank my students for making it memorable, and I thank my mentor for guiding me in every step of the way.
