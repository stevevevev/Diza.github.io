<h2> Experience</h2>

I was assigned to Math 5 for my first class. Together with my groupmates, Teachers Euniece, Scott, and Genesis, we were to teach “Prime and Composite Numbers” synchronously. The main thing that blew me away was how eagerly the students engaged with us, especially during our activities. Although online classes limited interactions, the grade 5 students remained very participative and it made me feel bad that we couldn’t do it face to face where their energy would definitely be more infectious.

Following our Math 5 experience, we were assigned for Science 8, next week, about “The Earth’s Crust”. As engaging as the grade 5 students were, the opposite was true to grade 8. Only 4-5 of the students kept participating, which really demotivated me since I thrive off of student participation. Furthermore, they usually just comment their answers which, admittedly, frustrated me because it didn’t feel like participation for me. I do realize that it is still participation, just in a form that I dislike. Despite the frustrations, I pushed through, and still appreciated the comments. What I like isn’t what my students will always want to do, and that is something that I have instilled in my mind since then.
